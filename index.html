About Me

I am 20 years old, currently studying at Capiz State University Dayao Satellite College Campus as a Second Year BSCS student. I want to be a great innovator, but I feel that most people reject or dislike those who don’t code manually. I’m not saying I don’t code manually, but the problems keep getting harder, and they aren’t even taught in school. So instead, See More... relying on other resources shouldn’t be shameful—that’s why I embrace open platforms where I can gain more ideas. To be straight to the point, I am an LLM enthusiast. I love experimenting with Large Language Models' capabilities. Most people don’t fully grasp their potential; they only see the threats and benefits. Artificial Intelligence is the future. However, I don’t know if AI developers have a contingency plan for potential risks. But since they are part of big tech, I trust that they don’t neglect security and safety measures. I’ve talked too much! What I’m trying to say is that I prefer fast progress rather than slowing myself down. I don’t want to wait for lectures because I’m sure that, at a deeper level, they don’t explicitly teach the proper application of concepts in real-world problems. Identification, classification, and quizzes can only help to a certain extent, but they don’t truly contribute to the growth of computer science. Instead, they often lower one’s self-esteem, leading to impostor syndrome. That’s all I have to say. As long as I stay dedicated and love what I do, I will always be confident in myself, embrace failure, and never neglect my studies as long as I live.
